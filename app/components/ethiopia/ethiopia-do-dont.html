Each country has their own faux. In Ethiopia there are a few to consider. How do I greet a person, how do I behave at religious sites? To make sure that the visit does not unknowingly ends in a disaster but in a success. So here is a list of all sorts of interesting information about Ethiopia you should know.

Do not walk past someone you know without greeting them Don’t rush through a greeting, it is important that people greet one another even if they do not speak the same language The eldest person is greeted first out of respect. Their age is thought to indicate wisdom, knowledge and experience Greeting strangers: common to shake hands, using the right hand or both hands Greeting friends: often put one another’s cheeks against each other around three times Greeting Muslims: Some Muslims may prefer not to embrace members of the opposite gender unless they are a close family member. Therefore, men should wait until a woman extends her hand first before extending his own hand for a handshake Use people’s titles to address them until they allow you to use their first name. Common titles include ‘Ato’ (Mr), ‘Woizerit’ (Miss) and ‘Woizero’ (Mrs). If a person with higher status or respect enters the room, it is polite to stand up Avoid using your left hand to passing objects, use the right hand or both hands together In some regions it is forbidden to take pictures

Invitation and payment

 It is expected to politely decline an invitation multiple times before accepting on the second or third time. The invitation shows hospitality and the refusal shows humbleness and that one is not greedy. That means that Ethiopians may refuse your offer out of politeness. Therefore, offer it more than once to give them the opportunity to accept something on the second or third attempt. It is inappropriate to ask to split a bill. The person who invites the other person to a meal is expected to pay at the restaurant.

Time

 punctuality is not that important. the pace of life is generally slower tasks may take a while to complete in Ethiopia, so plan more time and be patient if things last longer than expected.

Religion

 Don’t disrespect religion, be it Orthodox Christianity, Protestantism or Islam Don’t ask someone about their ethnicity.
