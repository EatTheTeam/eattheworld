The way the year is calculated in Japan is completely different than in any other country.
Every time a new emperor is elected, a new 'era' of reign is beginning. The first year of someone's era would be the first year of that emperor's time. For example, the year 2018 was called the thirtieth year of the Heisei-time.
But rest assured - the Gregorian calendar is also used in Japan and if you mention a year past 2000, everyone will understand what you are talking about. Though in day-to-day conversations, the japanese type of year is usually used.

Major Holidays

calendar_today New Year's

The new year is wildly celebrated within Japan, more than christmas. There are many traditions happening during new year and before, during and after this time, there are some free days.
Unlike China, the new year is celebrated on December 31st/January 1st.

calendar_today Golden Week

Golden Week is actually a series of several national holidays, but since they all happen so close together, all within the same week, it is like a whole week of holidays.
Golden Week takes place from the end of April to the start of May.

calendar_today The Emperor's birthday

In Japan, the birthday of the currently reigning emperor is a national holiday. Some birthdays of past emperors also get celebrated. It is a very important day for the japanese population.
For the year 2019, this day falls on December 23rd.

info When a holiday falls on a Sunday, it will automatically be moved to the next Monday afterwards!
