The way the year is calculated in Japan is completely different than in any other country.
Every time a new emperor is elected, a new 'era' of reign is beginning. The first year of someone's era would be the first year of that emperor's time. For example, the year 2018 was called the thirtieth year of the Heisei-time.
But rest assured - the Gregorian calendar is also used in Japan and if you mention a year past 2000, everyone will understand what you are talking about. Though in day-to-day conversations, the japanese type of year is usually used.

The time in Ethiopia is counted differently from many other Western African countries. They use a 12-hour clock, with one cycle of 1 to 12 and there are two cycles/ways to tell the time. The first one is from dawn to dusk (AM) and the other one is from dusk to dawn (PM). Most countries start the day at midnight while the day in Ethiopian starts with the sunrise, due to its location near the Equator it is almost always sunny. For example, 2 AM local Ethiopian time is called 8 AM in East Africa time. To convert between the Ethiopian clock and East clocks, one muss add (or subtract) 6 hours to the East African time.
