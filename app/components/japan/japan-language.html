The language spoken in Japan is Japanese. Japanese has its own set of characters for writing, and its grammar is very different from many other languages.
At its roots, it does have some things in common with Chinese, but those two languages are highly different. Surprisingly, a lot of Japanese words, especially for imported food, are the same as they would be in English.

Special Pronounciations

a - pronounced like "Ah", or like the A in aunt.
e - pronounced like "Eh", or like the E in ending.
i - pronounced liek "Eee", or like the I in information. o - pronounced like "Oh", or like the O in orbit.
u - pronounced like "Ooo", or like you would pronounce you.
oo, ou - like o and u, but spoken much longer. ji - pronounced like "Gee", or like you would pronounce jeans.
shi - pronounced like "Shee", or like you would pronounce Sheila.
In Japan, there is no differentiation between 'L' or 'R', they are the same letter and are pronounced the same way.
The Japanese 'R' sounds like a mixture of 'L', 'R' and 'D'.
There is a special angle in which you have to place your tongue while making the sound. It takes a lot of practice to get it correctly.
