Sushi is probably the most known traditional food in Japan. It is a variety of dishes that all contain mostly of raw fish and rice, usually mixed with ingredients like seaweed or vegetables. In history, putting the raw fish on top of rice was a means to preserve the fish and prevent it from spoiling. At some point, the Japanese began to eat the rice together with the fish and make a variety of different meals with it.

Sashimi is a special kind of sushi that is not served together with rice. Since it is just raw fish, it has to be very fresh. When served, the meat is sliced into extremely thin slices and often eaten with soy sauce.

Maki are a type of rolled sushi. There are many types like Futomaki, Hosomaki, Ehōmaki or Temaki, but in the most common way they are prepared on a large nori sheet (nori is a type of edible sea weed) with rice and long stripes of vegetables and such. They are then rolled and sliced to multiple pieces, which gives them their cylindrical shape.

You can eat ramen by first picking out the noodles and ingredients with chopsticks and then drink the soup out of the bowl.
